### Vision

### Mission

### Historical Timeline

### Basic Facts (verbatim from page)
- Parliament meets **on average three times a year**; meetings last **3–4 weeks**
- Proceedings in **English or Pidgin**; all documents (Bills, motions, questions, reports) in **English only**
- Parliament considers on average **13 Bills per year**
- Most Bills are Government Bills; occasional **private member's Bills**
- Sits **Monday to Thursday** — business set by Government; **Fridays = Private Members Day** (used mainly by Opposition)
- Typical sitting day: **9:30 am – 4:30 pm**
- Meetings are **open to the public**

**Descriptions extracted:**

- **Speaker** — Controls and supervises meetings; maintains order; ensures MPs act in accordance with Standing Orders. Elected by MPs from *outside* Parliament (not an MP).
- **Deputy Speaker** — Performs the Speaker's role in absence. Elected from *among* MPs.
- **Clerk** — Provides secretarial support to Parliament and Committees. Sits in front of Speaker during meetings; takes notes; advises Speaker and MPs on procedure.
- **Deputy Clerk** — Assists the Clerk.
- **Serjeant-at-Arms** — Assists Speaker to ensure Chamber rules are followed. Responsible for carrying the Mace, the symbol of the Speaker's authority.

### Named Standing Select Committees (from Standing Orders)
The following ten committees are constituted under Standing Orders:

1. **Public Accounts Committee** — examines government accounts; reviews Draft Estimates; Auditor-General serves as Secretary
2. **Parliamentary House Committee** — Members' terms and conditions; management of Parliament facilities; rules for Members' use of Parliament
3. **Bills and Legislation Committee** — reviews all draft legislation; examines subsidiary legislation; monitors motions requiring legislative action
4. **Constitution Review Committee** — reviews and advises on constitutional changes; examines proposed amendments; handles abuse/misuse of constitutional powers
5. **Foreign Relations Committee** — diplomatic relations; international treaties; appointment of Ambassadors; foreign assistance; foreign policy oversight
6. **Education and Human Resources Training Committee** — education and human resources development matters
7. **Health and Medical Services Committee** — health and medical services matters
8. **Police, National Security and Correctional Services Committee** — police, national security, and corrections matters
9. **Public Expenditure Committee** — budget expenditure review; value-for-money assessment; expenditure vs. policy alignment
10. **Environmental and Conservation Committee** — environmental change measures; national/provincial environmental legislation; international environmental obligations; carbon trading

### Key Procedural Rules Extracted
- Questions to Government: max **12 per sitting day**, not on Fridays or first sitting of a meeting
- Member may not ask more than **2 questions per question day**
- Notice for Government Bills: **3 clear days** before First Reading
- Notice for Private Members' Bills: **12 clear days** before First Reading
- Sitting bell rings for **2 minutes** before a division is taken
- Member suspension for grossly disorderly conduct: up to **14 sitting days**; fine up to **$100**
- Quorum of Select Committee: **one-third** of members (excluding Chairman)

## 4. Speaker's Office

**URL:** `parliament.gov.sb/about/speakersoffice/`  
**Title:** Speaker's Office | National Parliament of Solomon Islands *(note: "Solomomn" typo in title tag)*

### Content
- Speaker's position created under **Section 64** of the Constitution
- Elected every **4 years** per Order 5 of Standing Orders
- Role defined under **Section 65** of the Constitution: to preside over sittings
- Speaker elected from **outside** Parliament; Deputy Speaker from **among** MPs

> "The Speaker controls and supervises the meetings of Parliament. He maintains order in the House and ensures that MPs act and speak according to the Standing Orders (Rules of Parliamentary Procedure). There is also a Deputy Speaker who performs the role of the Speaker in his absence."

## 6. Departments

**URL:** `parliament.gov.sb/about/departments/`  
**Title:** Departments | National Parliament of Solomon Islands

### Introductory text (verbatim)
> "The various departments of the National Parliament of Solomon Islands collectively ensure the smooth functioning of the legislature by fulfilling specialized roles essential to its operations. Departments such as the Committee Secretariat and Procedures Department provide technical and procedural support, enabling effective legislative and oversight activities. The Finance & Accounts Department ensures financial accountability, while the Human Resources Department manages personnel to maintain a skilled and motivated workforce. The Hansard Department preserves transparency by recording and publishing verbatim reports of parliamentary proceedings, and the Sergeant-at-Arms Department ensures security and ceremonial integrity. Additionally, the Parliamentary Civic Education Department strengthens public engagement by promoting awareness of the Parliament's functions and fostering democratic participation. Together, these departments uphold the Parliament's mission of ensuring effective governance, accountability, and representation for the people of the Solomon Islands."
